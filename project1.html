KEDARNATH TEMPLE

ABOUT THE TEMPLE

Kedarnath is an ancient Hindu temple in Uttarakhand,India, dedicated to Lord Shiva and located at an altitude of 3,583 meters. It is part of the Char dham pligrimage and one of the 12 Jyotirlingas.The temple with the history linked to the Pandavas and reconstruction by Adi Shankaracharya. Kēdāranātha Temple is a Hindu temple, one of the twelve jyotirlinga of Śiva. The temple is located on the Garhwal Himalayan range near the Mandākinī river, in the state of Uttarakhand, India. Due to extreme weather conditions, the temple is open to the general public only between the months of April (Akṣaya Tritiya) and November (Kārtika Pūrṇimā, the autumn full moon). During the winters, the vigraha (deity) of the temple is carried down to Ukhimath to be worshiped for the next six months. Kēdāranātha is seen as a homogeneous form of Śiva, the 'Lord of Kēdārakhaṇḍa', the historical name of the region

HISTORY OF TEMPLE

The history of Kedarnath is blend of Mythological and Historical accounts,with a temple believed to have been built by the Pandavas and later reconstructed by Adi Shankaracharya in the 18th century.

Mythologica Origins:

The Kedarnath Temple is believed to have been originally built by the Pandavas after the Kurukshetra War as a penance for their sins. According to legend, they sought forgiveness from Lord Shiva, who disguised himself as a bull to avoid them. When the Pandavas recognized him, he vanished, leaving his hump behind, which is now worshipped in the temple as the Shivling

Historical development

Kedarnath Temple is part of the Char Dham pilgrimage and is situated at an altitude of over 11,000 feet in the Himalayas. The temple is accessible only during certain months due to extreme weather, typically from April to November. During the winter months, the deity is moved to Ukhimath for worship

Construction and Architectural Significance

The current structure of the temple is attributed to Adi Shankaracharya, who is said to have rebuilt it in the 8th century to revive Hindu pilgrimage practices. The temple is constructed using massive interlocking stone slabs without mortar, showcasing advanced engineering techniques that have allowed it to withstand harsh weather conditions and natural disasters.

ABOUT THE BHEEM SHILA

To the devout, Kedarnath Bhim Shila is a profound symbol of protection by the Divine, its imposing visage evoking reverence. For adventure seekers and nature lovers, it offers soul-stirring Himalayan vistas and an unforgettable journey of self-discovery. Scientists have long studied the unique properties underlying its balance, while conservationists watch over this geological treasure with care.

During the catastrophic floods of 2013 that wreaked havoc in Kedarnath, Bhim Shila miraculously withstood the ravages of nature. While the towering waters swallowed everything around it, this immortal sentinel stood firm, shielding the temple just as legend foretold.

Its resolute presence offered hope to the affected populace a reminder that faith and the human spirit can overcome even the direst tragedy. For pilgrims and visitors today, making the steep climb to Bhim Shila‘s rocky embrace is an act of devotion, the spectacular views and spiritual charge leaving a lasting imprint on the soul.

ABOUT THE SHIVLINGAM

The presiding image of Kedarnath in the form of lingam is more triangular in shape with a pedestal 3.6 m (12 ft) in circumference and 3.6 m (12 ft) in height. There is a small pillared hall in front of the temple, that has images of Parvati and of the five Pandava princes. There are four temples around Kedarnath itself, namely- Tungnath, Rudranath, Madhyamaheshwar, and Kalpeshwar which form the Panch Kedar pilgrimage sites.
The first hall inside Kedarnath Temple contains statues of the five Pandava brothers, Krishna, Nandi, the vehicle of Shiva and Virabhadra, one of the guards of Shiva. Statues of Draupadi and other deities are also installed in the main hall.An unusual feature of the temple is the head of a man carved in the triangular stone lingam. Such a head is seen carved in another temple nearby constructed on the site where the marriage of Shiva and Parvati was held. Behind the temple is the samādhi mandir of Adi Sankara

TRANSPORT SERVICES

BY ROAD

If you choose to travel by taxi then there are options available i.e., private taxi and sharing taxi. For family or group travel private taxis are good options, but if you are travelling solo or with 2 members then it is advisable to choose sharing taxi.

BY TRAIN

As there is no railway station in Kedarnath. The nearest railway stations to Kedarnath are Rishikesh and Haridwar. Both the stations are well-connected to major cities across India.

BY AIR

The nearest airport to Kedarnath is the Jolly Grant Airport in Dehradun. This airport is well-connected to major cities like Delhi, Mumbai and Ahmedabad.
